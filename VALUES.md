# Care Guardian — Values Statement

## Your family's care. Your family's data. No one else's.

---

Care Guardian was built for a specific moment in a family's life: the moment when someone you love is diagnosed with dementia, and everything that follows — the medical decisions, the legal paperwork, the financial maze, the daily logistics, the emotional weight — lands on your shoulders.

We built this app because we've been in that moment. And we believe the tools available to family caregivers don't reflect the seriousness of what's being asked of them.

These are our values:

### Your data belongs to you

Care Guardian encrypts everything on your device. Not on our servers — we don't have servers. Not in a cloud database — there is no cloud database. Your parent's medication list, their diagnosis, their behavioral logs, their financial information, their advance directive — all of it is encrypted with a key that only your family knows. If you lose the key, we cannot recover your data. That's not a limitation. That's the promise.

We will never monetize your data, because we never have your data.

### Caregiving is not a checklist

A traditional to-do app tells you that care is 73% complete. That's a lie. You don't complete caregiving. You attend to it, day after day, with varying degrees of success and exhaustion.

Care Guardian distinguishes between tasks you do once (execute the power of attorney — done forever) and tasks you do repeatedly (reconcile medications every six months, monitor sleep patterns every night). The Foundation bar fills up. The Care Pulse bar fluctuates. Together they tell the honest story: *how set up are we, and how well are we keeping up?*

### Dignity is not a feature — it's a design constraint

The person with dementia is not a passive subject of your care plan. They are a person with preferences, fears, joys, and — especially in the early and middle stages — opinions about their own care.

Care Guardian gives the care recipient their own access to the app. In Independent mode, they can see everything — their legal documents, their financial situation, their care team's notes. They can export their own data. They can submit self-reports describing how they feel in their own words or their own voice.

When the time comes — and the family will know when — the administrator can switch to Supported mode with one tap. The view simplifies. The financial details disappear. The self-report stays. The transition is gentle, reversible, and controlled by the family, not by an algorithm.

We put the After Death checklist in the sidebar, not the tab bar, because a family will use this app for years before that feature is needed. Surfacing it every day would be cruel. But it's there when it's needed, because someone has to handle those tasks too.

### Accuracy over coverage

When we say a Medicaid income limit is $2,901 per month, we mean Oregon's OSIPM income standard, cited to the specific ORS section, with the specific phone number to call if you have questions. We don't say "check your state's limits." We tell you the limit.

We started with Oregon because we could get Oregon right. Every program name, every threshold, every citation, every phone number has been verified. We would rather cover one state accurately than fifty states approximately.

When we expand to other states, each state package will be built to the same standard: maintained by people who know the law in that jurisdiction, updated when thresholds change, and never generated by guesswork.

Generic mode exists for families in states we haven't covered yet. It provides honest, universal guidance without pretending to know your state's specific rules.

### Your care team is not our user base

Care Guardian supports the full spectrum of people involved in dementia care: the family member who holds power of attorney, the sibling who helps on weekends, the home health aide who works the overnight shift, and the care recipient themselves.

Each person sees what they need and nothing more. The aide sees the medication grid, the incident log, the shift schedule, and the emergency plans — the tools of their job. They don't see the family's financial situation, the estate plan, or the Medicaid spend-down strategy. That boundary isn't about distrust. It's about appropriateness.

When a care worker logs an incident, they can't delete it. When they record a medication administration, it's permanent. Accountability protects everyone — the care recipient, the family, and the care worker.

### Sync without surveillance

Care teams need to share data across devices. Most apps solve this by routing everything through a central server that the company controls. We solved it differently.

Care Guardian syncs through a file in your family's own cloud folder — Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud, whatever you already use. The file is encrypted end-to-end. The cloud provider sees an encrypted blob. We see nothing, because we're not in the loop.

For families who want full control, we publish a sync server you can run on your own hardware. It's 169 lines of code with zero dependencies. It stores encrypted blobs and serves them back. It never sees your data. You can read every line of the source code and verify this yourself.

The sync passcode is never stored on disk. It exists in memory during your session and vanishes when you lock the app. If someone steals your phone, they get an encrypted vault they cannot open.

### We don't track you

No analytics. No telemetry. No usage metrics. No crash reporting. No A/B testing. No behavioral data collection. No advertising. No partnerships with data brokers. No "anonymized" data sharing.

We don't know how many people use Care Guardian. We don't know which features are popular. We don't know if you opened the app today. We are structurally incapable of knowing these things, because the app never contacts us.

This makes us worse at product development and better at earning your trust. We believe that's the right tradeoff for an app that holds your family's most sensitive information.

### Open by default

Care Guardian is a single-file React application. The entire source code — every feature, every encryption routine, every data structure — is in one file you can read. There is no hidden server component. There is no proprietary backend. There is no API that phones home.

If you stop trusting us, you lose nothing. Your data is on your device, encrypted with your key, in a format you can export. You can fork the source code and maintain your own version. You can hire a developer to audit our encryption. The app works offline, forever, with no dependency on us or anyone else.

### Built for the hardest years of your life

Dementia caregiving is measured in years, not weeks. The person you're caring for will change. Your family dynamics will shift. Care workers will come and go. Medical situations will escalate. Financial resources will deplete.

Care Guardian is designed to endure alongside you. The care plan adapts as abilities decline. The access tiers adjust as autonomy narrows. The incident log builds a longitudinal record. The visit prep summary aggregates months of observations into a document you can hand a specialist.

We can't make caregiving easier. But we can make sure you never lose track of what's been done, what still needs to happen, and who's responsible for what.

That's all an app should try to do.
